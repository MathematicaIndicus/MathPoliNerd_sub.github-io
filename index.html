About Me

Anurag Goswami

Mathematician · Geopolitical Enthusiast · Writer

Hello! I'm Anurag, a second year B.Sc. student currently majoring in Mathematics. I have a keen interest in knowing about things, mainly 'Traditional-Mathematics' like Number Theory, Linear Algebra, Differential Equations and real life 'Modelling' related stuffs. I started this blog for a very specific reason - to cover my 'Backlogs'. I know it might sound funny, but this is the only way I can complete my overdue pile of work - By writing about it! and trying to teach others.

But then you might wonder...Why "Politics"? Politics has been a subject of fascination for me since a long time. But since I am starting my journey in Mathematics, I thought it would be interesting to explore how mathematical concepts can be applied to understand political phenomena. So here I am, trying to bridge the gap between these two seemingly different fields. Though there is no hard and fast rult that I will only write about these two subjects, I am open to exploring other topics as well. The main goal of this blog is to share my thoughts and ideas with others. Hope you'll find it interesting and informative!

Btw, when I'm not writing or studying, you'll find me reading philosophy, running, or losing at chess.

Fields of Interest

Mathematics & Formal Methods

From number theory to combinatorics, I explore how formal mathematical structures can models illuminate social and digital phenomena. Expect proofs, puzzles, and beautiful abstractions.

Political Science & Democracy

Electoral systems, legislative behaviour, comparative politics — I analyse institutions and political actors with the same precision I'd bring to a mathematics problem.

🎯

Game Theory

Strategic interaction is at the heart of politics. I use game-theoretic frameworks to understand why actors behave the way they do under uncertainty and competition.

📊

Statistics & Data

What does the data actually say? I dig into political and economic datasets to separate signal from noise, and to test theories against empirical reality.
